A Sacred Legacy

The Goti Adarian stands as more than just a place of worship—it is a living testament to the resilience and devotion of the Parsi-Zoroastrian community. For over two centuries, this sacred space has been the heart of spiritual life in Surat, Gujarat.

Our temple is home to the sacred fire that has burned continuously for generations, tended by dedicated priests who maintain the ancient traditions passed down through millennia. This eternal flame represents the light of Ahura Mazda and serves as a beacon of hope and guidance for all who enter our doors.

Today, the Goti Adarian continues to play a vital role in preserving Zoroastrian culture, hosting religious ceremonies, educational programs, and community gatherings that strengthen the bonds between past, present, and future generations.

Our Mission

  • Maintain the sacred fire and uphold ancient traditions
  • Foster community unity and cultural preservation
  • Educate future generations about Zoroastrian heritage
  • Provide spiritual guidance and support to our community

Temple Features

Sacred Fire Hall

The central chamber where the eternal flame burns, maintained according to ancient Zoroastrian traditions and attended by qualified priests.

Prayer Halls

Dedicated spaces for individual and communal prayers, designed to provide a peaceful environment for spiritual reflection and worship.

Community Hall

A gathering space for community events, educational programs, and celebrations that bring together members of all ages.

Library

A collection of sacred texts, historical documents, and educational materials preserving Zoroastrian knowledge and heritage.

Education Center

Classrooms and facilities for religious education, language classes, and cultural programs for children and adults.

Guest Facilities

Accommodation and dining facilities for visiting community members and guests participating in religious ceremonies.
